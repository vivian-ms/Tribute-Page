Notable Career Moments

Lakers 8

  • June 26, 1996
    Kobe is drafted

    Kobe Bryant is selected by the Charlotte Hornets with the 13th pick of the 1st round of the NBA Draft

  • July 11, 1996
    Kobe becomes a Lakers

    Kobe is traded to the Los Angeles Lakers for Vlade Divac

  • June 19, 2000
    First championship

    21-year-old Kobe wins his first NBA title. Kobe averaged 15.6 points, 4.2 assists, and 4.6 rebounds in the 2000 NBA Finals, including a 26-point, 10-rebound performance in the deciding Game 6 against the Indiana Pacers

  • June 15, 2001
    Second consecutive title

    Kobe and Shaq asserted themselves as the NBA's most dominant duo again, beating Allen Iverson and his Philadelphia 76ers in the Finals. Kobe finished with 26 points and 12 rebounds in Game 5 to win his second NBA title

  • June 12, 2002
    Three-peat

    Behind Kobe's 26.8 points per game, the 2002-03 Lakers becomes only the fifth team in NBA history to win three consecutive championship after sweeping the New Jersey Nets in 4 games

  • December 20, 2005
    62 points

    Lakers beat the Dallas Mavericks 112 - 90 behind Kobe's 62 points. Kobe played 32 minutes and scored 62 points, outscoring the entire Mavericks team by one point (62 - 61) at the end of the third quarter

  • January 22, 2006
    81 points

    Lakers beat the Toronto Raptors 122 - 104 in a comeback victory behind Kobe's 81 points, the most points in a game after Wilt Chamberlain's 100 points in 1962

Lakers 24

  • March 16, 2007 - March 23, 2007
    Four straight games with 50+ points

    Kobe put up four straight games of at least 50 points against the Portland Trailblazers (65), Minnesota Timberwolves (50), Memphis Grizzlies (60), and New Orleans/Oklahoma City Hornets (50) in March 2007

  • June 14, 2009
    Championship No. 4 (the first without Shaq)

    Kobe led the Lakers over the Orlando Magic in five games, scoring at least 30 points in all but one game in the 2009 Finals

  • June 17, 2010
    Five rings

    Kobe averaged 28.6 points and 8 rebounds per game in the 2010 NBA finals against the Boston Celtics, claiming his fifth Larry O'Brien Trophy

  • April 12, 2013
    Beginning of the fall

    Kobe tears his Achilles tendon against the Golden State Warriors. With the Lakers fighting to get the eighth and final playoff spot in the Western Conference, Kobe began playing almost 48 minutes each game. His injury came after playing seven consecutive quarters and at least 40 minutes for seven consecutive games

  • December 14, 2014
    No. 3 on NBA's All Time Scoring List

    During the Lakers's 100-94 road victory over Minnesota, Kobe hit a pair of free throws with 5:24 remaining in the 2nd quarter to pass Michael Jordan to claim the No. 3 spot

  • November 29, 2015
    Retirement annoucement

    Kobe posted a first-person poem titled "Dear Basketball" on the Players' Tribune website announcing that 2015-16 would be his final season

  • April 13, 2016
    Final game

    Kobe scored 60 points on 22 of 50 shooting in his final game to help the Lakers defeat the Utah Jazz 101-96

Team USA

  • August 24, 2008
    First Olympic gold

    Team USA invited Kobe to the Olympic team for the first time in 2008, where he captained the 'Redeem Team' to gold, defeating Spain 118-107 in the final

  • August 12, 2012
    Second Olympic gold

    Kobe was one of five players from the 2008 Olympic team to return in 2012, where they scored a record 156 points against Nigeria in the first round and defeated Spain 100-78 in the gold-medal game

Career Highlights and Awards

  • 5x NBA champion (2000–2002, 2009, 2010)
  • 2x NBA Finals MVP (2009, 2010)
  • NBA Most Valuable Player (2008)
  • 18x NBA All-Star (1998, 2000–2016)
  • 4x NBA All-Star Game MVP (2002, 2007, 2009, 2011)
  • 11x All-NBA First Team (2002–2004, 2006–2013)
  • 2x All-NBA Second Team (2000, 2001)
  • 2x All-NBA Third Team (1999, 2005)
  • 9x NBA All-Defensive First Team (2000, 2003, 2004, 2006–2011)
  • 3x NBA All-Defensive Second Team (2001, 2002, 2012)
  • 2x NBA scoring champion (2006, 2007)
  • NBA Slam Dunk Contest champion (1997)
  • NBA All-Rookie Second Team (1997)
  • Naismith Prep Player of the Year (1996)
  • Numbers 8 & 24 retired by Los Angeles Lakers

Kobe Bryant watching both his No. 8 and No. 24 jerseys being revealed up in the rafters. Kobe becomes the only player to have two numbers (8 and 24) retired by the same franchise (Los Angeles Lakers)
